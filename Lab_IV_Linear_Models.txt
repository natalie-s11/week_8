1. What makes a model "linear"? "Linear" in what?
A model is linear because it is linear in the parameters, B coefficients. This means tha tthe model is a linear combination of all the coefficients, even if the variables are transformed.

2. How do you interpret the coefficient for a dummy/one-hot-encoded variable? (This is a trick question, and the trick involves how you handle the intercept of the model.) There's further explanation at the end of this document, if needed.
The coefficient for a dummy/one-hot-encoded variable is interpreted relative to the one dropped aka the baseline category when intercept = True. For example, if the Bronx is the baseline and the coefficient for Manhattan is 107, this means that being in Manhattan increases the price by $107 compared to the Bronx.
